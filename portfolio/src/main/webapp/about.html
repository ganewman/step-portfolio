Childhood

My full name is Gabrielle Newman and I'm 20 years old. I grew up in Brooklyn, New York. My family moved around the city a bit, but I lived in NYC my whole life until I left for college. I went to high school in Manhatten, and taking the subway to school every day was a really cool experience. Well, except for when train delays would make me late for class.

Computer Science

I got into computer science when I took a required introductory class as a sophomore, and haven't looked back since. I love puzzles and problem solving, and for me, that's what CS is all about! Within CS, the two areas I've found myself the most interested in are theoretical CS and operating systems. They're very different, but what can I say? I just love to write proofs and code in C.

School

I currently attend the University of Massachusetts Amherst, where I just finished up my sophomore year. I'm a double major in Computer Science and Mathematics (with a concentration in computing). I'm also in the Commonwealth Honors College at UMass, part of which means I'm on track to write a thesis in my senior year. The thought of it is honestly pretty scary, since I know it will be a lot of work. However, I also think it'll be a really great learning experience. UMass can be really fun, since it's so big and in such a beautiful area (western Massachusetts). However, it can also be hard to find myself so far from home. Still, I've made a lot of great friends in my time there and wouldn't trade the experience for anything.

Languages

I took Japanese as my foreign language in high school, as well as an intensive course for a semester in college. My Japanese actually isn't half bad! I can write fairly quickly and neatly, though my reading is much better than my listening comprehension. I'd really love to move to Japan for a few months someday to try and improve my fluency. Right now, I'm hardly at the level of an elementary schooler. However, my first language was actually Hebrew! I unfortunately don't speak it very well anymore, but I was totally fluent as a child. I also used to be able to read and write fairly well. Now, my listening comprehension is OK but that's about all. I wish I had time to take a Hebrew language course... I think it would come back to me pretty quickly!

Pets

My favorite animals in the entire world are cats! I just think they're so cute, fluffy, and cuddly. I used to have a cat named Scout, who I raised from the time he was a kitten. Unfortunately, since I left for college, he hasn't been living with me. Instead he's been staying with my cousin. But he's still a very cute and happy kitty!
